MINDVIS 3D - PROJECT STUDY NOTES
--------------------------------------
Subject: How sound actually travels through the brain (And how we built it)
Source: YouTube (Neuroscience basics), Research papers, and jamming with Gemini/AI.

OVERALL LOGIC: 
I realized early on that most visualizers are "dumb." They just make a shape bounce to volume. If I'm building a brain, it has to think like a brain. Sound isn't instant; it's a sequence.

1. THE ENTRY POINT (The "Tail")
- Research: Sound enters via the Cochlear nerve -> Brainstem (specifically the Medulla/Pons).
- My Note: This is the raw data. It’s the "vibration" phase.
- Code Implementation: Built the Brainstem as a central pillar. Stage 1 signals start here. Use Gold/White sparks because the data hasn't been "filtered" by the lobes yet.

2. THE GATEKEEPER (The Thalamus)
- Research: Signals hit the Medial Geniculate Body (MGB) in the Thalamus.
- My Note: This is like a network switchboard. It decides what's important.
- Code Implementation: Placed a "Core" anchor in the middle of the brain. Everything flows through here first before hitting the surface.

3. THE CONSCIOUS EAR (Temporal Lobe / A1)
- Research: First stop in the cortex is the Primary Auditory Cortex.
- My Note: This is where you actually *realize* you're hearing a song. 
- Code Implementation: This is our "Stage 1 End / Stage 2 Start" handoff point. Everything hits the Temporal Lobe before fanning out to the rest of the brain.

4. THE CHAIN REACTION (The "Dispersal")
This was the hardest part to get right. Sound doesn't just stay in the ear. It spreads:
- Motor Cortex (Front/Top): Reacts to Rhythm/Bass. (That's why we tap our feet).
- Amygdala (Deep Core): This is the "Chills" center. Reacts to the Mids (melodies).
- Prefrontal Cortex (Very Front): This is the "Analyzer." Looks for patterns/complexity. Reacts to Highs.
- Visual Cortex (Back): Imagery. How we "see" music in our head.

--------------------------------------
TECHNICAL DISCOVERIES (The "Aha!" moments)
--------------------------------------

* THE "SUN BRAIN" PROBLEM:
Originally, I used Additive Blending. It looked like a giant glowing lightbulb. AI pointed out that real tissue blocks light. Switched to standard blending + 0.3 transmission. Looks way more "meaty" and biological now.

* YAKOVLEVIAN TORQUE:
Human brains aren't symmetrical! The right side is slightly shifted forward. I added a "Torque" multiplier (0.97 vs 1.03) to the math. It’s a tiny detail, but it’s why the model looks "real" instead of like a plastic toy.

* THE GPU BYPASS:
React is too slow for 60fps audio bars. Use Refs + CSS Variables. Bypasses the virtual DOM. Standard industry trick for high-end dashboards.

* THE "STEREO MIX" HACK:
Browser security is a nightmare. Chrome blocks us from "listening" to Spotify. Solution: Use the system loopback (Stereo Mix). It tricks the browser into thinking the speakers are a microphone.
